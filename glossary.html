Quantum Computing Glossary

Fundamental Concepts

Qubit |ψ⟩

The basic unit of quantum information. Unlike classical bits that are either 0 or 1, a qubit can exist in a superposition of both states.

|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, where |α|² + |β|² = 1

Superposition

A quantum principle where a qubit exists in multiple states simultaneously until measured. The probabilities of each state are given by the squared magnitudes of the amplitudes.

H|0⟩ = (|0⟩ + |1⟩)/√2

Entanglement

A quantum phenomenon where two or more qubits become correlated such that the state of one instantly affects the other, regardless of distance.

|Φ+⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2

Measurement

The act of observing a quantum state, which causes the wavefunction to collapse to one of the basis states with probability determined by the amplitudes.

P(|0⟩) = |α|², P(|1⟩) = |β|²

Interference

The phenomenon where quantum amplitudes combine additively. Quantum algorithms use interference to amplify correct answers and cancel wrong ones.

Decoherence

The loss of quantum properties due to interaction with the environment. This is one of the biggest challenges in building practical quantum computers.

Quantum Parallelism

The ability of quantum computers to evaluate a function on all possible inputs simultaneously through superposition.

n qubits → 2ⁿ states in parallel

Quantum Gates

Hadamard Gate H

Creates superposition by transforming |0⟩ to an equal superposition of |0⟩ and |1⟩. Essential for initializing qubits for quantum algorithms.

H = (1/√2) [[1, 1], [1, -1]]

Pauli-X Gate X

The quantum equivalent of the classical NOT gate. Flips |0⟩ to |1⟩ and vice versa.

X = [[0, 1], [1, 0]]

Pauli-Y Gate Y

A single-qubit gate that applies a 180° rotation around the Y-axis of the Bloch sphere.

Y = [[0, -i], [i, 0]]

Pauli-Z Gate Z

Applies a 180° rotation around the Z-axis. Leaves |0⟩ unchanged and flips the phase of |1⟩.

Z = [[1, 0], [0, -1]]

CNOT Gate CX

A two-qubit gate where the target qubit flips if the control qubit is |1⟩. Essential for creating entanglement.

|c⟩|t⟩ → |c⟩|t ⊕ c⟩

Phase Gate S

Applies a 90° (π/2) phase shift to the |1⟩ state. Foundation for more complex phase rotations.

S = [[1, 0], [0, i]]

T Gate T

Applies a 45° (π/8) phase shift. One of the fundamental gates in fault-tolerant quantum computation.

T = [[1, 0], [0, e^(iπ/4)]]

Quantum Algorithms

Deutsch-Jozsa

First quantum algorithm showing exponential speedup. Determines if a function is constant or balanced with one query.

Classical: O(2ⁿ), Quantum: O(1)

Grover's Algorithm

Quantum search algorithm providing quadratic speedup for unstructured search problems.

Classical: O(N), Quantum: O(√N)

Shor's Algorithm

Factoring algorithm providing exponential speedup. Poses a threat to current RSA encryption.

RSA-2048: Classical ~10²¹ ops, Quantum ~10⁷ ops

Quantum Fourier Transform

The quantum analog of the discrete Fourier transform. Foundation for many quantum algorithms.

QFT|x⟩ = (1/√N) Σₖ e^(2πixk/N)|k⟩

Quantum Phase Estimation

Estimates the eigenvalue of a unitary operator. Fundamental to chemistry simulation and factoring.

Measurement & Error Correction

Bloch Sphere

Geometric representation of a single qubit state. Any pure state can be represented as a point on this sphere.

|ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩

Density Matrix ρ

Mathematical representation of quantum states, including both pure and mixed states.

ρ = Σᵢ pᵢ|ψᵢ⟩⟨ψᵢ|

Quantum Error Correction

Techniques to protect quantum information from decoherence and errors. Uses redundancy through entanglement.

Fault Tolerance

The ability of a quantum computer to operate reliably even with noisy qubits and imperfect gates.

Important Quantum States

Bell States

Maximal entangled two-qubit states. The four Bell states form an orthonormal basis.

|Φ±⟩ = (|00⟩ ± |11⟩)/√2, |Ψ±⟩ = (|01⟩ ± |10⟩)/√2

GHZ State

Multi-qubit entangled state generalization of Bell states. Used in quantum networking and sensing.

|GHZ⟩ = (|00...0⟩ + |11...1⟩)/√2

W State

A different type of multi-qubit entangled state with exactly one qubit in |1⟩.

|W⟩ = (|100⟩ + |010⟩ + |001⟩)/√3

Cat State

Named after Schrödinger's cat, this is a superposition of two coherent states with opposite phases.

|Cat⟩ = (|α⟩ + |-α⟩)/√2
