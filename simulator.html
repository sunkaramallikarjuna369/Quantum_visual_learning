📌 What is a Qubit?

Think of a light switch that can be both OFF and ON at the same time!

|0⟩
OFF (like 0)
can become
|1⟩
ON (like 1)

A qubit starts at |0⟩. Gates change it to |1⟩ or create superposition (both at once!).

1️⃣ Hadamard Gate (H)

H Creates Superposition!
Takes a qubit from a definite state (|0⟩ or |1⟩) and makes it both at the same time! This is called superposition.

What H does:

|0⟩
always OFF
──[H]──
( |0⟩ + |1⟩ ) / √2
50% OFF, 50% ON!

🧪 Try It: Click H to see what happens!

|0⟩
100%

Qubit starts at |0⟩ (100% chance of measuring 0)

2️⃣ Pauli-X Gate (X)

X Flips the Qubit!
Just like a NOT gate in classical computing! Changes |0⟩ to |1⟩ and |1⟩ to |0⟩.

What X does:

|0⟩
OFF
──[X]──
|1⟩
ON (flipped!)

🧪 Try It: Click X to flip!

|0⟩
100%

Start: 100% |0⟩

3️⃣ CNOT Gate (Controlled-NOT)

⊕ Creates Entanglement!
This is a 2-qubit gate! If the first qubit is |1⟩, it flips the second qubit. If the first is |0⟩, nothing happens. This creates quantum entanglement!

What CNOT does:

|00⟩
q[0]=0, q[1]=0
──[CNOT]──
|00⟩
q[0]=0, no flip

But if first qubit is 1:

|10⟩
q[0]=1, q[1]=0
──[CNOT]──
|11⟩
q[1] flipped!

📚 Other Common Gates

Pauli-Y (Y)

Y
Rotates the qubit around the Y-axis. Changes both the state and the phase!

Pauli-Z (Z)

Z
Only affects the |1⟩ part - adds a minus sign! Called a "phase flip". |0⟩ stays the same.
|1⟩
──[Z]──
-|1⟩
phase flipped!

Phase Gate (S)

S
Adds 90° of phase. |1⟩ becomes i|1⟩ (i is the imaginary number).

T Gate (T)

T
Adds 45° of phase. Used in more advanced quantum algorithms.

🔗 Now Let's Build Circuits!

How Circuits Work

A circuit is a series of gates applied to qubits from left to right. Each horizontal line is one qubit.

Circuit Diagram:

q[0]: ──[H]──────────
q[1]: ──────[CNOT]──

This means: Apply H to q[0], then CNOT to q[0] and q[1]
