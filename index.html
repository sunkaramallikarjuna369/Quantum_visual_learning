Step-by-Step Tutorial

1

Understanding Qubits

A qubit is described by |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, where |α|² + |β|² = 1. The squared magnitudes give measurement probabilities.

Adjust probability of |0⟩:

|0⟩: 100% |1⟩: 0%
2

The Hadamard Gate

Creates superposition: H|0⟩ = (|0⟩ + |1⟩)/√2 and H|1⟩ = (|0⟩ - |1⟩)/√2

Try applying H in the simulator above!

3

CNOT Gate - Creating Entanglement

CNOT flips the target qubit if the control is |1⟩. Combined with H, it creates Bell states - maximally entangled pairs.

4

Quantum Parallelism

n qubits represent 2ⁿ states simultaneously. A register of 30 qubits can hold 1 billion values at once!
